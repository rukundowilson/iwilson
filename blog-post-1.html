November 20, 2024 Personal Story

Hi, I'm Wilson Rukundo!
Crafting technical solutions in the heart of Kigali City.

From the moment I could hold a pencil, I've been creating. At just 6 years old, my journey into programming began. Only two weeks after my dad brought home a brand-new HP Elitebook 8460p, I had already taught myself HTML and was writing my first program. Innovation has been my passion ever since.

Drawing Has Always Been My First Love

Drawing has always been my first love, even more than computers. Surprisingly, it shares many similarities with coding; both require a keen eye for detail, a sense of color and balance, and the patience to correct mistakes until everything still bugs. This artistic perspective has fueled my passion for coding, making every line of code a brushstroke in my digital masterpiece.

The Joy of Problem-Solving

Solving puzzles and tackling mind games like jigsaw puzzles, word games, logic challenges, crosswords, and Sudoku has always been my comfort zone. I vividly remember being 8 years old, unable to put down my mom's cell phone because I had a Sudoku game I could. Even then, problem-solving was my passion, and it has driven me ever since.

Ready to Make an Impact

I'm ready to embark on a career in software engineering. If your team needs a dedicated and skilled developer to drive meaningful change, look no further—I'm your ideal candidate.

How I Built My First Real-World App Using Node.js & MySQL

Building my first production-level application using Node.js and MySQL taught me more than any tutorial ever could. I had to deal with authentication, database design, security concerns, and real user requirements.

One of the biggest lessons was the importance of clean database schema design: a good schema makes every feature faster to build. Another lesson: never underestimate validation and error handling. The moment real users interact with your system, every missing check becomes a bug.

My Journey From Blender Experiments to 3D Thinking

Before I ever wrote serious code, I was experimenting with Blender. I didn't realize then, but 3D modeling taught me essential problem-solving habits: breaking big tasks into smaller meshes, flipping perspectives, dealing with constraints, and iterating through imperfections.

These same skills now help me debug code, design UI flows, and optimize systems. Creativity and logic aren't opposites — they fuel each other.

Wilson Rukundo

Software Developer based in Kigali, Rwanda. Passionate about building innovative solutions and sharing knowledge with the community.
